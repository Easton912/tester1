Now for the stage in MM2 that easily has my favourite music from the game, Crash Man's stage! While the stage itself is definitely nothing fancy.... heck, this stage is pretty dull... the music is definitely a lot better. XD The stage design leaves a lot to be desired with slow moving platforms that follow a rail, cheating birds, and really, REALLY slow ladder climbing when there are several screens worth of ladders. The enemies are few and far between here, too... the stage does not even look all that nice, but eh, stage design was definitely where the later Mega Man games excelled. 
Development Speed: The team developed Mega Man 2
in their spare time, as it was initially not 
approved by Capcom management due to the poor 
sales of the first game.Difficulty Options: 
International versions added a "Normal" mode, 
which allowed for double damage against bosses,
whereas Japanese and later releases were locked
to the harder difficulty.Iconic Music: The 
soundtrack, particularly the "Dr. Wily's Castle 1" 
theme, is frequently cited as one of the best in 
video game history.


  

Crash Man was built by Dr. Wily specifically for 
combat in his revenge against Mega Man using the 
designs of Bomb Man and Guts Man as a base, with 
high speed and agility, the use of high explosives
as primary weapons, and clad in a thick armor that 
can effectively withstand explosions.
